01 / 04  ·  Plurals · First Listen

Welcome to İstanbul.

It's a big city. It's busy. And there's always more than one of everything — taxis, bridges, voices. Today we start listening to how Turkish handles that.

🎙 Audio script

In Turkish, when something becomes more than one, we add a small piece at the end. The good news? It's way easier than in many languages. In English you have 'cats', 'buses', 'children'… lots of exceptions. In Turkish? Let's start by just feeling how it works. Listen:

Araba→Arabalar
Otel→Oteller
Arkadaş→Arkadaşlar
Merhaba→Merhabalar
Genç→Gençler
Teşekkür→Teşekkürler
Just listen for now. Some plurals sound heavier, some lighter. Notice it — don't analyse it yet. Turkish is very musical like that.

03 / 04  ·  Deep Vowels

The "A" Echo

Some words carry a deep, open sound. Look at the last vowel — it comes from the back of the mouth. The plural echoes that depth with an A.

The last vowel of Araba is a. Banka → a. Durak → a. Deep vowel, deep echo. It's a rhythmic beat — -lar, -lar, -lar.
