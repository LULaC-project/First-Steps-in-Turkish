The First Contact
🏪
Entering a shop
Polite, universal
Merhaba
🤜🤛
With your crew
Casual, street-style
Selam!
🎙 Audio
In Turkey, greetings matter. You don't usually walk into a place silently. Even in a small shop, you greet first. Your first key to any conversation is Merhaba. It's the universal classic — polite and always right. But if you're with your close crew, you go with the street-style version: Selam!
2
Boosting the Vibe (-lar / -ler)
MERHABA → MERHABALAR
SELAM → SELAMLAR
BASE Merhaba
+
SUFFIX -lar = Merhabalar 🎉
Adding -lar / -ler makes your greeting warmer, more enthusiastic —
like "Hello everyone!" instead of a flat "Hello."
🎙 Audio
You already know a lot of expressions from the previous week. We can add -lar or -ler to some greetings to reinforce the meaning. Merhaba becomes Merhabalar. Selam becomes Selamlar. Both are correct, but adding that extra piece makes you sound warmer and more enthusiastic.

4
The Morning Rule — Günaydın
🌅
08:00 AM · Bakery
Fresh simit, first coffee
Günaydın!
🚕
Early morning taxi
Getting a ride
Günaydın!
☀️
Günaydın
"May the day be bright"
Before 10 AM
🎙 Audio
So, what happens when you walk into a bakery at sunrise? Here is a city rule: while Merhaba is never wrong, in the early hours, Günaydın always wins! It literally means "May the day be bright." If it's before 10 AM, skip the Merhaba and go with a bright Günaydın!

Afternoon & Evening Flow

Good evening
🌙 Café Scene — Evening
🚶
İyi akşamlar.
You, entering
🧑‍🍳
İyi akşamlar.
Waiter responds
🎙 Audio
As the sun moves, our words change. In the bright afternoon, when leaving a shop or hanging up a phone, we say İyi Günler (Have a good day). But once those neon lights start to glow, we switch to İyi Akşamlar. It's the melody of the city matching the sky.
